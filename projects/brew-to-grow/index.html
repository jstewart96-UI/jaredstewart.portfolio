Brew to Grow

Connecting coffee waste to community gardens

Role UX Designer & Researcher
Timeline 3 months
Tools Figma, User Research, Prototyping
Sustainability Mobile App Community User Research

The Problem

Every day, coffee shops across the country discard hundreds of pounds of used coffee grounds. Meanwhile, community gardens struggle to find affordable, nutrient-rich compost for their plants. This disconnect represents both an environmental waste and a missed opportunity for community connection.

Through initial research, I discovered that:

  • An average coffee shop produces 10-15 pounds of used grounds daily
  • Most grounds end up in landfills, contributing to methane emissions
  • Community gardens often lack access to quality compost
  • There's no efficient system connecting these two communities

Research & Discovery

I conducted comprehensive user research to understand both sides of this equation and identify opportunities for meaningful intervention.

1

Coffee Shop Interviews

Interviewed 12 coffee shop owners and managers to understand their waste management challenges and willingness to participate in community programs.

2

Garden Community Survey

Surveyed 45 community garden coordinators to understand their compost needs, pickup capabilities, and interest in a digital platform.

3

Competitive Analysis

Analyzed existing waste management and community sharing platforms to identify gaps and opportunities for innovation.

Solution Design

Based on my research findings, I designed a mobile app that creates a seamless connection between coffee shops and community gardens. The solution focuses on simplicity, reliability, and community building.

Reflection

This project taught me the importance of understanding multiple user groups when designing for community impact. The challenge of balancing the needs of coffee shop owners (who need efficiency) with community gardens (who need reliability) pushed me to think more holistically about user experience.

Key learnings included the value of in-person research, the importance of building trust in community platforms, and the power of simple solutions to complex environmental problems.
