Brew to Grow

Redirecting coffee waste for a sustainable future

Role UX Designer & Researcher
Timeline 3 months
Tools Figma, User Research, Prototyping
Sustainability Mobile App Community User Research

The Problem

Coffee shops and other businesses often generate large amounts of spent coffee grounds that go to waste every day. This app addresses this problem by providing a platform where individuals can connect with businesses that are willing to donate their used coffee grounds. This initiative not only reduces waste, but also promotes sustainability.

The Solution

This platform revolutionizes sustainability efforts by seamlessly connecting users with local coffee shops to collect and recycle used coffee grounds. With intuitive onboarding, users effortlessly contribute to waste reduction while fostering community engagement. By quantifying their impact through metrics like CO2 emissions saved and personal milestones, users gain insights into their environmental footprints and inspire others to join the movement towards a greener future.

Onboarding

The onboarding screens offer new users a seamless introduction to the app's mission and functionality. Users are guided through the process of collecting used coffee grounds from local businesses. By emphasizing simplicity and clarity, the screens empower users to get started with ease, encouraging them to join the movement towards a more sustainable future.

Process

User research, including surveys and usability tests, influenced the design and features of the app. The research aimed to understand user needs and preferences for coffee ground waste collection and gardening, with the goal of providing a seamless and engaging user experience.

Research

I conducted user research through surveys and in-person interviews to gain better insight into the target audience backgrounds, goals, and potential frustrations. The data provided by the research allowed me to create a persona that facilitated effective decision making and resulted in improved user experience and development for this platform.

Solution Design

With the data gathered from research, I created digital wireframes and prototypes and had 4 potential users provide feedback for further development.

Main User Flow

This diagram shows a snapshot of how users navigate through the app. From logging in or creating an account, searching for businesses, and reserving a bag(s) for pick-up.

Design Iterations

User feedback collected from usability testing provided the necessary critiques to implement changes along the development process. I was able to address areas of concern where users notice aspects that were too cluttered, hard to read or navigate, etc. Initially, the first iteration was cluttered with text which caused users to feel overwhelmed, while in the second iteration I simplified the layout resulting a cleaner, more intuitive interface for improved user navigation and readability.

Reflection

This project taught me the importance of understanding multiple user groups when designing for community impact. The challenge of balancing the needs of coffee shop owners (who need efficiency) with community gardens (who need reliability) pushed me to think more holistically about user experience.

Key learnings included the value of in-person research, the importance of building trust in community platforms, and the power of simple solutions to complex environmental problems.
