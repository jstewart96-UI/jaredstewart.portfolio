Peet's Coffee Mobile App

How a Small Improvement Streamlined a Core Function for Increased Usability

Role UX Designer
Timeline 1 month
Tools Figma, Usability Testing
Mobile App Usability Coffee UX Improvements

"How do I check in?" "Where's the scan button?"

Analyzing the most common problem users face with this app

Overview

Working in a coffee shop offers valuable insights beyond enjoying excellent coffee. It provides the unique opportunity to receive first-hand feedback from customers who express their frustrations with the Peet's Coffee mobile app. Additionally, I have access to our loyalty metrics through Power BI, giving me a comprehensive view of how many guests check-in with the app against the store's overall customer count. As a UX designer, I have taken these concerns to heart and used them to inform a redesign of the Peet's Coffee Mobile App, ensuring that customer feedback and data-driven insights shape the user experience.

The specific problem was in the drink customization interface, where users had to navigate through multiple screens to make simple modifications to their orders. This created unnecessary complexity for what should be a quick, intuitive process.

Problem

Many users find it difficult to locate the QR code for scanning within the app. Previous and current iterations prioritize visual elements and promotional content, which can obscure the QR code's placement and make it less intuitive to find quickly. This difficulty in accessing a core functionality hampers the app's usability, detracts from the seamless customer experience that the app aims to provide, and hinders our ability to increase loyalty metrics.

Solution

This case study explores the potential impact of our company's app redesign on loyalty metrics and overall user satisfaction. The redesign is informed by user feedback and aims to address their main concerns. It represents a step towards a more user-friendly, streamlined app and a more engaging user experience. The project's goal was to redesign the home screen of Peet's Coffee's mobile app to enhance user experience, streamline functionality, and simplify access to key features.

Sourcing the Problem

In order to understand the problem users were facing with the app, I took note of customers who verbally expressed difficulty locating the "scan to check in" button. However, this was not a streamlined or consistent approach as there was the variable of those same customers not coming back in. To counter this issue, I asked 20 random customers if they've had difficulty with the app, in terms of the check in process and navigation.

Analyzing the past to enhance the future: Observations

One previous version of the app excelled at creating a visually appealing and brand-consistent interface. However, its design, featuring a central coffee cup graphic and promotional image, may have prioritized aesthetics over functionality. While the personalized greeting and intuitive navigation bar were strengths, the interface could have been streamlined to focus more on key user interactions, such as scanning for rewards and checking point statuses.

While the current app version is visually appealing and consistent with the Peet's brand, there are areas for improvement in navigation and UI design. Improving the visibility and accessibility of key features such as the QR code scanner and rewards points, along with ensuring better contrast for interactive elements, could significantly enhance the user experience.

Comparing Competitor Apps

Now, these call outs are based purely on my own observations and are not informed from direct user research. That being said, the Starbucks app, while functional and visually appealing, could benefit from better utilization of the top section and addressing the truncated labels to be easier to read. The clear labeling, sizable design, and position of the "Scan in Store" button enhance its visibility and usability, making it easy for users to locate and interact with it.

The Dunkin app, on the other hand, presents a more populated top section but could also benefit from increased personalization and dynamic content, as well as some more simplified and streamlined areas. Although it is a more "active" home page that also presents more clutter and potential confusion with users i.e. there is a lot of things to look at.

Both apps were used to inform my design and decision making while redesigning the Peet's app. The Starbucks and Dunkin apps have strong designs but also present areas of opportunity. By identifying these areas, I was able to incorporate my observations into redesigning the Peet's app, enhancing its user experience and interface.

A Comprehensive Redesign

By combining first-hand feedback from Peet's users and conducting competitive audits of the Starbucks and Dunkin apps, I streamlined the Peet's app home page to prominently display the QR code for check-ins, added a rewards progress bar, and made "Tap to Scan" the primary call-to-action button. The new design integrates user feedback and successful elements from competitor apps to enhance the user experience. If fully realized, these improvements are expected to boost loyalty metrics by providing a seamless and user-friendly app interface.

Iterations and iterations

The design iterations of the Peet's Coffee app home screen showcase a thoughtful and progressive approach to enhancing the user experience. The process began with the initial concept of prominently displaying the QR code for easy access, coupled with a minimalistic progress bar. User feedback highlighted the need for more detailed information and better visual feedback, leading to the second iteration, which introduced a more detailed progress tracker with specific milestones. As iterations continued, the design incorporated a "Just for You: Rewards and Offers" section to personalize the user experience.

Further iterations refined the color scheme to balance sophistication and readability, ensuring high contrast between elements for optimal legibility. The inclusion of a "Details" button in the final iteration responded to user requests for more information about their points and rewards, providing a more engaging and informative interface. Each iteration was informed by user testing, competitive analysis of industry leaders like Starbucks and Dunkin, and a focus on creating a seamless, intuitive, and visually appealing user experience.

Conclusion and what I learned

The redesign of the Peet's Coffee app was a transformative experience that underscored the importance of user-centered design and competitive analysis. Through this project, I gained invaluable insights into how iterative design, grounded in real user feedback, can significantly enhance user satisfaction and engagement. By actively incorporating feedback from Peet's users and conducting competitive audits of industry leaders like Starbucks and Dunkin, I was able to identify key areas for improvement and implement effective solutions.

Key improvements included prominently displaying the QR code for easy check-ins, adding a detailed rewards progress bar to motivate users, and introducing a clear "Tap to Scan" call-to-action button. These changes not only streamlined the app's interface but also made it more intuitive and engaging. The process highlighted the impact of small, thoughtful changes on the overall user experience and demonstrated the importance of a cohesive and visually appealing design.

This project taught me the value of thorough user research and the need to stay attuned to industry trends and best practices. It reinforced the significance of continuous feedback loops and iterative testing to refine and perfect the user interface. Moving forward, I will continue to leverage user insights and competitive research to drive innovation and create seamless digital experiences. The experience has made me more adept at balancing user needs with business goals, ultimately aiming to boost loyalty metrics and user engagement through well-designed digital solutions.
